Our Philosophy

Marentis Labs exists to close a gap the governance industry has spent two decades widening. Organisations have never spent more on governance, yet severe failures continue at the same rate. Our white paper sets out the full evidence and traces the pattern to its source. The conclusion holds across sectors, jurisdictions, and regulatory regimes. Governance fails because the assumptions beneath the strategy go unchallenged until the consequences arrive.

The research identifies three structural defects in how boards are governed. Oversight arrives episodically, in scheduled meetings that miss the moments where strategy actually turns. Decision-making rewards consensus, which quietly suppresses the dissent a board most needs to hear. And challenge survives only where an individual chooses courage, because the system itself requires it of no one. Marentis Labs corrects all three. We install challenge as a permanent function, active between board cycles and built to surface what consensus conceals.

This is Strategic Governance as a Service. We act as a continuous, adversarial challenge function for the board, stress-testing every framework, examining every assumption, and pressure-testing every control before a regulator, an acquirer, or a crisis does it first. A board that has been genuinely challenged is a board far harder to surprise, to deceive, and to catch unprepared.

The Founder

Owen Vallis

Managing Director & Founder

Marentis Labs Ltd

Strategic Risk Executive

Financial Services Group Chief Risk Officer & Independent Non-Executive Director

Owen Vallis brings two decades of senior institutional governance and strategic risk management to the work of stress-testing corporate strategy and investment decisions. He built that perspective at the centre of Tier 1 global finance, across Credit Suisse, J.P. Morgan, and Morgan Stanley. As the first Group Chief Risk Officer for SICO Bank, he built the holistic risk management function from inception and reported directly to the Board.

Direct board experience sharpens that institutional depth. Owen has served as a Non-Executive Director on the Audit and Risk Committee of a government statutory body reporting to Parliament, and as Chairman of Active Prospects, where revenue tripled during his tenure.

Owen has sat on both sides of the boardroom table, accountable to a board as a senior executive and providing oversight as a non-executive director. That dual vantage gives him the instinct to locate the flawed assumptions and hidden vulnerabilities that quietly undermine performance.
