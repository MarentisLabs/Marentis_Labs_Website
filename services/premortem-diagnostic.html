Pre-Mortem Diagnostic: Killing the Project to Save It

A high-impact, short-duration engagement to identify and assess the potential failure points of a critical strategic initiative *before* significant resources are committed.

Systematically Dismantling Optimism Bias

The Pre-Mortem Diagnostic is a structured, independent, and confidential forum designed to systematically dismantle optimism bias and groupthink within a leadership team. It helps surface the complex, often unstated, assumptions that represent the most significant threats to a project's success.

The Diagnostic Process (2-3 Weeks)

  1. Scoping & Alignment: A session with the project sponsor to define the scope, confirm desired outcomes, and agree on a clear definition of "failure".
  2. Intelligence Gathering: Confidential review of core project documentation (investment case, project plan, financial models) to understand the core logic and stated assumptions.
  3. The Pre-Mortem Workshop: A facilitated session where stakeholders imagine the project has already failed catastrophically and work backwards to identify every plausible cause.
  4. Synthesis & Critique: We synthesize the workshop output into a concise Critique Memorandum, analyzing underlying patterns and identifying the most critical unexamined assumptions.
  5. Executive Debrief: A confidential session to present the findings and facilitate a discussion on actionable next steps.

Core Outcomes

  • Identification of previously unexamined strategic assumptions.
  • A clear action plan for mitigating high-impact, low-probability risks.
  • Increased clarity and commitment among the leadership team before launch.

Engagement Profile

Best For:

De-risking major projects, transformations, or investments prior to launch.

Typical Duration:

Rapid Execution: **2-3 Weeks**

Key Deliverable:

A concise Critique Memorandum and an Executive Debrief with actionable recommendations.
