Service Architecture

Strategic Governance as a Service

Four tiers structured as a progression from initial assessment to deep, board-level governance partnership. Every engagement deploys the Adversarial Methodology — Red Team Protocol, Risk Simulation Lab, and Pre-Mortem Diagnostic — calibrated to the tier.

A Continuous, Cumulative Governance Partnership.

Traditional governance advisory is episodic. A firm engages a consultant, receives a report, and manages governance internally until the next incident triggers the next engagement. Institutional knowledge is lost between engagements.

SGaaS is structured as a continuous, principal-led function delivered on a retainer architecture. It complements internal audit, risk, and compliance — it does not replace them — and provides the adversarial challenge the Three Lines Model was never designed to deliver.

The four tiers are designed as a progression, not a menu. The Diagnostic builds the institutional knowledge that makes the Retained tier effective; the Retained tier creates the conditions under which Embedded becomes a natural evolution; the Pre-Exit tier turns governance maturity into a defended exit valuation.

T3

Embedded SGaaS

Permanent Board-Level Challenge Function

The Embedded tier provides everything in the Retained tier, plus permanent board-level presence. The SGaaS principal attends board and committee meetings as a contracted Independent Board Observer (or Mandated Advisor to the Risk or Audit Committee) with contractual challenge authority. The principal does not vote and owes no fiduciary duties — a deliberate architectural choice that preserves the independence on which the challenge function depends.
